# One Lantern

One Lantern is a horror game where you play as someone exploring forests at night (yeah, I don't know who would come up with that, honestly). The problem is, these aren't ordinary forests! They're forests where strange things happen, so you'll encounter various enemies and have to survive for a certain amount of time to complete each level.

Use your hearing to survive and conserve energy—you'll need it!

## How to play

You have only two controls:

- *Turn on flashlight:* Left-click

- *Pause game:* Escape key

To survive, you must listen for approaching monsters; if you think one is coming, shine your light on it until it disappears completely. Once a certain amount of time has passed (displayed in the top right corner), you will complete the level.

When enemies vanish, there is a chance they will drop items to help you on your journey!

## What tools I used

- *Game Engine:* Godot Engine 4.7
- *Drawing App:* Aseprite
- *Sound Effects:* Audacity

## How I made it

I used the Godot Engine to develop my video game and all its underlying logic. I started with simple textures created in Aseprite in just a couple of minutes, gradually upgrading the graphics as I developed the game and its mechanics.

Here are the steps I took to implement the mechanics:
1. Mouse-based character controls

2. Lighting system

3. Enemies

4. Enemy spawning

5. Enemy despawning

6. Item spawning

7. Level interfaces, etc.

After finishing the logic, I began improving the game's textures until they were reasonably presentable. Finally, to enhance the experience and make the gameplay feel fairer (and less random), I added sound effects for monster spawns so you know exactly when they appear!
